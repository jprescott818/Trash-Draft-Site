Trash Draft: Features

Schedule & Class Info

Day 1: PITCH DAY (9a-12:30p PST on Zoom)

Students will gather on Zoom to pitch out their ideas in a "writer's room"-type environment led by Instructor. Pitches can be as short as a log-line, as long as a page but should focus on your premise, your main character, your genre/tone and the catalyst that thrusts the story.

*If trying to decide between multiple ideas, students are encouraged to bring all (in short form) so the room may help determine what's the best use of the writers' week.

At the end of this Zoom session, writers should expect to have all the materials necessary for breaking story.

Day 2: OUTLINE DAY (on your own, no Zoom)

Using the brainstorm from yesterday's zoom, writers flesh out their script ideas into structure (Save the Cat feature structure; 8 sequence structure, etc). Additional outline worksheets and other supplemental materials are provided and reaching out to Instructor directly for help as well as their fellow writers via email is encouraged. 

Day 3-6: SCRIPTING ACT ONE (on your own, no Zoom)

Writers pursue their Trash Drafts with daily check-ins via email from Instructor which includes what specific page count to hit for the day (averaging 6-8 script pages/day) as well as detailed guidance for what "part of the story" they should be focusing on:

         • Cold Opens / "Opening image" (pg. 1-2)
         • Theme Stated (~pg. 5)
         • Set-up (pg. 1-12)
         • Catalyst (~pg. 12)
         • Debate (~pg. 12-24)
         • Break into Act 2 (~pg. 25)

Day 7: ZOOM CHECK-IN (9a-12pm PST)

Optional Zoom for writers to check-in with Instructor for how their draft is going.

Day 8-13: SCRIPTING ACT TWO (on your own, no Zoom)

Writers continue to pursue their scripts, breaking into the 2nd act. Daily email check-ins from Instructor on what specific page count to hit (continuing 6-8 pages/day) as well as detailed guidance on what "part of the story" they should be focusing on:

         • B-Story (~pg. 30, optional)
         • "Fun and games" of Act 2A (~pg. 25-45)
         • Mid-point (~pg. 55)
         • Bad Guys Closing In (~pg. 55-60+)

Day 14: ZOOM CHECK-IN (9a-12pm PST)

Optional Zoom for writers to check-in with Instructor for how their draft is going.

Day 15-20: SCRIPTING ACT THREE (on your own, no Zoom)

Home stretch-- and your hardest week as a writer, yet. Writers continue to pursue their scripts, wrapping up their 2nd Act, breaking into the 3rd Act and pursuing their finales. Daily email check-ins from Instructor on what specific page count to hit (continuing 6-8 pages/day) as well as detailed guidance on what "part of the story" they should be focusing on:

    • Continue Bad Guys Closing In (~pg. 60-74)
    • All Is Lost (~pg. 75)
    • Dark Night of the Soul (~pg. 76-84)
    • Break into Act 3 (~pg. 85)
    • Finale (~pg. 86-109)
    • Finale Image (pg. 110 / wherever you end)

Day 21: ZOOM CHECK-IN (9a-12pm PST)

Final optional Zoom check-in for writers for how their draft is going.

Day 22-27: POLISHING (on your own, no Zoom)

Writers spend their week polishing their work, trying to highlight a specific 30-page section they'd like to bring to the table-read. Writers are encouraged to reach out to Instructor as they re-read their progress.

Day 28: TABLE READS

Students present a key 30-page section of their script to be read by both fellow writers and professional actors. Writers will receive detailed notes on their completed draft from Instructor 1-2 weeks after class is wrapped.
