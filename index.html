EMAIL TRASH DRAFT
Do I need to have written before?

Trash Draft is not a "Pilot 101" course (though those are always on the horizon!). Instead, it's a place for writers who know the basics of screenplay formatting and 3-act structure to hit the ground running on a new project. This is so that every student who signs up for Trash Draft is not only on the same relative level to best help each other with notes and brainstorming, but that students don't have to hit road blocks beyond the time crunch to finish their projects.

Trash Draft writers do NOT need to have been hired professionally as a writer previously (and I can assure you, this is not one of those writing classes where we all go around and share our writer credits in ways that will undoubtedly trigger jealousy and make us wanna go back to grad school and/or give the UCB $4 million dollars.)

Are classes in-person or online?

Every Trash Draft class is on Zoom unless otherwise noted.

How does it all work?

For the ORIGINAL TRASH DRAFT, writers meet twice on Zoom: 

Day 1 is PITCH DAY where the class of 6 students + Instructor meet to pitch out their ideas for Trash Draft. If students have multiple ideas they're weighing, they'll present each log line and we'll vote in a "Gong Show" style. 

Days 2-7 is WRITING ON YOUR OWN. Students will receive daily reminders, check-ins and other instruction via email from Instructor for how to tackle their script getting as specific to the page count and story section (For ex: "Today is pgs. 10-15/17; you should be ending your Act 1 break, focusing on your mid-point and threading in your B-stories, etc").

Students are encouraged to use both Instructor and their fellow students as "life lines" during the scripting process. Think of it like a writer's group made just for you, just for one week. 

Day 8 is MARATHON READ-THROUGHS. Students gather again on Zoom to read their Trash Draft scripts. If needed, outside actors will be invited to perform their work. Every script is followed with 15-20 minutes of rapid-fire notes + a follow-up from Instructor to answer any lingering questions before pursuing a 2nd draft.

And that's it! You now have the 1st draft of a script you didn't have a week earlier. 

What happens after I miss a class?

All class sessions are recorded for convenience, but since this is a workshop dependent on participation from you and your fellow students, attending even if you've not completed the work is highly encouraged. 

What if I need help with a writing project that isn't listed?

1:1 sessions are a great way to get specific care for a project (TV pitch, short film, feature, etc), especially if you have an upcoming deadline.
