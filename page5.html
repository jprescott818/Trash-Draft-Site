Trash Draft: Rewrite

Schedule & Class Info

Day 1: MARATHON READ-THROUGHS: GENERAL

Students "rip off the band-aid" by diving immediately into presenting the script they'd like to rewrite. If students require additional actors to read, they must reach out to Instructor no less than one week prior to class. 

Every read-through is followed by a detailed 20-30 minute notes session led by instructor. Session wraps with a 20-30 minute lecture about tips and tricks for approaching your rewrite. 

Day 2-6: REVISING - GENERAL

Writers work on their own (no Zoom) to apply the notes given during their marathon read-throughs. Writers are "never alone" as they work, using email and phone to connect with Instructor as well as pinging their fellow students. 

Day 7: MARATHON READ-THROUGHS: CHARACTERS, THEMES AND PREMISE

Another round of read-throughs with the revisions made to the script, this time with an eye for deepening the theme; streamlining the premise; and strengthening your characters. Each read-through will have a 20-30 minute notes session to follow. If students require additional actors to read, they must reach out to Instructor no less than one week prior to class. 

Day 8-13: REVISING - SPECIFICS

Writers apply the notes given in the last read-through to their scripts on their own (no Zoom). 

Day 14: MARATHON READ-THROUGHS: PUNCH-UP AND POLISH

The last round of read-throughs. By this point, you and your fellow writers are familiar with each other's scripts and can spend this read-through focusing on punching up jokes for comedy; utilizing environments, backstories and other tools for heightening drama; or other means of polishing to get the script in the strongest state.

AFTER CLASS

Students are able to send their finished draft to Instructor for notes up to 4 weeks after class has wrapped. Notes will be turned around via email.
