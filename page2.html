Trash Draft: August

Schedule & Class Info

Sat, Aug. 16th: 9a-12p PST (Day 1: PITCH DAY)

Students will gather on Zoom to pitch out their ideas in a "writer's room"-type environment led by Instructor. Pitches can be as short as a log-line, as long as a page but should focus on your premise, your main character, your genre/tone and the catalyst that thrusts the story.

*If trying to decide between multiple ideas, students are encouraged to bring all (in short form) so the room may help determine what's the best use of the writers' week.

At the end of this Zoom session, writers should expect to have all the materials necessary for breaking story.

Sun, Aug. 17th: OUTLINE DAY (on your own, no Zoom)

Using the brainstorm from yesterday's zoom, writers flesh out their script ideas into structure (Dan Harmon Story Circle, Save the Cat or the "Story Checklist" provided by Instructor). Additional outline worksheets and other supplemental materials are provided and reaching out to Instructor directly for help as well as their fellow writers via email is encouraged.

Mon, Aug. 18th-Sat, Aug. 23rd: SCRIPTING (on your own, no Zoom)

Writers pursue their Trash Drafts with daily check-ins via email from Instructor which includes what specific page count to hit for the day (averaging 5-7 script pages/day) as well as detailed guidance for what "part of the story" they should be focusing on (cold opens; act one cliffhangers, etc; your protagonist's "want"; the first "turn" to stumble your protagonist; injecting the b-story, etc). All throughout, writers will never feel "alone" as they write, using email to ping Instructor as they work, or reaching out to class email.

Sun, Aug. 24th: 9a-2:30p PST (MARATHON READ-THROUGH)

Writers can submit their finished trash drafts up until 8:59am PST. If possible, outside actors will be called in to read from their scripts. Notes follow every read. Zoom is recorded.
